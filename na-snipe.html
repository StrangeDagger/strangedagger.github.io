NA Snipe Bird Habitat Suitability


I conducted a multi-criteria analysis to assess the optimal habitats for the North American Snipe within the designated study area. Using ArcGIS Pro, I applied Zonal Statistics and Raster Calculator to integrate several environmental factors, such as slope, proximity to water sources, and vegetation cover.

The process began by collecting Digital Elevation Models (DEMs) and reclassifying them based on elevation ranges that favored the snipe's habitat preferences. I also used Buffer Analysis to create buffer zones around water bodies, as the North American Snipe is typically found near wetlands or streams.

To refine the habitat analysis further, I employed Slope and Aspect Tools to determine terrain suitability, focusing on gentle slopes that the snipe prefers. Finally, I performed Weighted Overlay Analysis to combine all factors, producing a suitability score for each area. The result highlighted regions with the highest likelihood of being suitable for the North American Snipe, providing a clear visual map of optimal conservation areas.

Tools:

  • ArcGIS Pro
  • NLCD
